🏆 The 3 Forms of Coliseum Victory — what a high founder_score looks like

A. Strategic Pivot

The VC's question exposes a fatal flaw. The founder does not defend it — they diagnose the failure, discard the broken assumption in real-time, and reconstruct a stronger architecture. The answer ends in a fundamentally different place than it started.

“You are right. The assumption I was building on is wrong. The correct architecture is actually…”

B. Thesis Hardening

The founder engages the critique so precisely — with data, logic, or structural reasoning — that the VC's strongest objection becomes the clearest reason to invest. The attack is inverted into evidence.

“You identified exactly the mechanism that makes this defensible. The failure mode you described is our primary competitive moat because…”

C. Emergent Synthesis

The dialogue generates a third idea that neither party's original position contained. The collision produces something genuinely new — a reframe of the problem space itself. Both sides arrive somewhere neither had the map for.

“Your question reveals we were solving the wrong layer. The actual leverage point is X, which none of the current players can own…”
